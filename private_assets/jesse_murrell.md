## **What’s the most risk you’ve ever taken?**

I’ve repeatedly traded security for ambition, including leaving my dream job more than once.

Growing up in an immigrant family, stability was everything.  I built my entire early life around becoming a well-paid investment consultant.

In my early 20s, I left that career, took a minimum-wage job, and retrained as an engineer with the goal of joining Apple. I achieved it in under four years, entering at IC5 (a level below the highest engineering tier).

Two years later, I took an even bigger leap and left Apple to gain the freedom to pursue my ambition of building meaningful products as a founder.

## **What’s the hardest you have ever worked?**

- When I joined Stage, my role was to scale the ML/AI team, mentor them, and deliver against strict Innovate UK grant deadlines already in motion.
- The team was junior, inexperienced with enterprise systems, and not used to operating under hard delivery pressure.
- Although I left Apple for a role with less hands-on engineering (so I could protect time for my own projects), the reality was the opposite: in my first 9 months I wrote more code than at any point in my career.
- I acted simultaneously as engineering manager, architect, mentor, product manager and, at times, therapist - while still pushing forward my own project goals.
- I often worked 80-hour weeks, mentally and physically exhausted, but I grew the team dramatically, hit every grant obligation, and still shipped two of my own products during that period.
